## Possible solutions
### Student Housing
Your first option should be [STW Berlin](https://www.stw.berlin/en/housing/), the official dorms for students.
Finding an accommodation in Berlin is challenging, but since you're an exchange student, it 
might be easier.
Every semester they reserve a number of spots for Erasmus / exchange students; as long as you apply some time in advance, you should be able to get a place there. They have the cheapest rooms. Pro: cheap, you're living with other students, (possibly) many events, solid contract covering your entire exchange period. Contra: you might be assigned to dorms a bit far away from the centre, you will probably be sharing the bathroom and the kitchen (some students prefer having a single apartment). I strongly recommend this option.
Also, if you are in an "emergency" situation, you can go to Studentenwerk and explain your situation, they might find something.

### Free market
Another option is searching in the free market. I recommend these websites (in order of importance): WG Gesucht, ImmobilienScout24, Immowelt, InBerlinWohnen, WGCompany. The listings receive hundreds of messages a few minutes after being published. Therefore, you would need to spend a lot of time checking the website and sending the messages (preferably in German). For the WGs (shared flat) take into account that usually they invite you to a visit of the flat, in order to decide whether to accept you. On the other hand, you could find a flat closer to the uni or in the clubbing areas, if you are into that. Pro: (possibly) choice of the area/place where to live. Contra: can be more on the expensive side, needs a lot of time, possible scams.

### Agencies
Otherwise you could try with some agencies such as HousingAnywhere, Wunderflats, Spotahome. Should not require a flat visit. You are gonna pay more than average but it is faster in comparison with the previous option. Pro: relatively fast process, reliable contracts. Contra: expensive, possible hidden fees.
